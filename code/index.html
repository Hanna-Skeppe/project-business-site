Come and Stay in the Woods

Welcome to Pine Tree landscape cabins where nature and imagination run wild

Even though you might be a world away from home, at the Pine Tree landscape cabins we welcome you like family. Let us take you away from the stresses and demands of everyday life. Living among the tall trees in the pine forest of Northern Sweden has never been so easy.

Gaze out at Sweden’s spectacular nature. Watch the Northern Lights illuminate the sky above you and your snowmobile, or take a family fishing trip and learn all about the berries you pick up along the way. One thing’s for sure at Pine Tree cabins, you’ll be inspired by your surroundings.

Whether you’re looking for an adventure, a relaxing break or simply to wind down in nature, Pine Tre landscape cabins invites you to experience nature, and our restaurant and surroundings, in the most unique and memorable way you can imagine.

FAQ

How do I get to the cabins?
From the train-station you could go bu buss nr 9 to magical forest bus stop, and then there is a 20 minutes walk from there. Follow the signs. If you go by car, you take road 22 to Ramsjö and then road 55 towards Aspan. After 5 km you will see our sign by the road. Or you could order transfer from us from the train-station (see below).
Can I use the Internet in the cabins?
Yes. We have high speed wireless Internet in all our cabins and also free wifi in the main building.
Can I book a transfer to/from the cabins?
Yes we have a bookable pick up service from the train-station. Make sure to write in your booking that you wish to order a transfer. It is also possible to contact us directly to book a transfer from the train-station, but we need to know minimum a week before you arrive. Transfer costs 150 SEK per person.
Is there bed linen?
Bed linen is not included in the price, but you can choose to add bedlinen when booking. It costs 200 SEK per person and week. 100 SEK for children under the age of 6.
What activities are there?
We have a wide range of activities for all ages. Join one of our guided tours where we show you the magic surroundings in the forest. Or order a prepared luch or "fika" to enjoy on outings of your own. Or perhaps come along on a northern lights night tour and take your own spectacular images. We also have a lot of other outdoors and family-oriented activities for the kids. Hire a boat and take a fishing trip with the family perhaps? Or just explore the serene nature around the cabins and pick berries.
Are the cabins accessible for the disabled?
Not all of our cabins are easily accessible by weelchair, but a few of them are. There are som nature trails that can be accessed witch weelchair or babycarriage. Our restaurant is accessible for people with disabilities. We reccomend that you contact us before booking just to make sure that accessibility-matters will work out as best as possible for you.
